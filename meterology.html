Famous Meteorologists: There discoveries:

 

William Ferrel

  Ferrel worked to develop theories that explained atmospheric circulation at mid-latitudes in great detail.

John Dalton

 He figured out that all matter is actually made up of small particles. Made Fahrenheit.

Gabriel Fahrenheit

 He figured out how temperature is expressed and how it can be expressed in Fahrenheit.
 

Alfred Wegener

 He was most famous for his theory of Continental Drift.

      Facts About Weather:
  • A bolt of lightning can travel at up to 136,000mph!
  • Temperature can be measured in Celcius or Fahrenheit 
  • Ice in the Antarctic is more than 2.5 miles thick in places.
  • Meteorology comes from the Greek words ‘meteoros’ and ‘logia’ which means ‘in the sky’ and ‘study’
  • The cause of weather comes from the sun and the moisture.

 Education needed to become a meteorologist:

To become a meteorologist you must attend at least 4 years of college to get a Bachelor of Science Degree. Although many meteorologists attend more years to achieve a Master of Science or maybe even a doctoral degree. People who major in Meteorology and Atmospheric Science need a strong background in science, math and computer skills (just like in this class!!). In high school, students should take earth sciences, physics, chemistry, and mathematics through at least pre-calculus. Penn State has a very good program for meteorology even though it is very tough to get in and is very expensive. If you are looking to become a meteorologist start studying now because you have a long road ahead of you.
